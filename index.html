Instructional Design Methodology

My approach to creating effective learning experiences

ADDIE Model

I employ this systematic framework for complex learning projects requiring thorough planning:

  • Analysis: Needs assessment and audience analysis
  • Design: Learning objectives and assessment planning
  • Development: Creating learning materials and media
  • Implementation: Delivery and learner support
  • Evaluation: Measuring effectiveness and impact

Example: Metso Outotec Compliance Training utilized ADDIE for systematic development of regulated content.

SAM (Successive Approximation Model)

For projects requiring rapid development and frequent stakeholder input, I use this agile approach:

  • Preparation: Information gathering and alignment
  • Iterative Design: Rapid prototyping and feedback
  • Iterative Development: Incremental building with testing

Example: The Diversity Movement Training Transformation leveraged SAM for quick iterations and stakeholder input.

Scenario-Based Learning

I develop authentic scenarios that place learners in realistic situations where they must apply knowledge and make decisions. This approach increases engagement, context relevance, and transfer to the workplace.

Example: "The Modern Leader" simulation in Skillieo's leadership program includes 20 branching scenarios with realistic leadership challenges.

Microlearning

When appropriate, I create focused, bite-sized learning units that target specific skills or knowledge gaps. This approach supports just-in-time learning, spaced repetition, and mobile accessibility.

Example: Metso Outotec Compliance Training includes microlearning refreshers on key compliance topics for ongoing reinforcement.
